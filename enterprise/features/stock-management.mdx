## Overview

The Stock Management section provides tools for tracking and managing your inventory across all locations. You can view stock levels, record deliveries, transfer stock between locations, make adjustments, and perform stock counts.

## Viewing Stock Levels

### Accessing Stock Information

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Stock Management">
  Click "Stock" in the main navigation menu to access the stock management section.
</Step>

<Step title="View Stock">
  Use the **search box at the top** to find stock items. Type your search (e.g., product name, site, or outlet) and results will appear below, showing stock items with their current levels.
</Step>

<Step title="View Stock Details">
  Click on any stock item to see detailed information including:
  - Current stock level
  - Unit of measure
  - Par level (target stock level)
  - Recent movements
  - Stock value
</Step>
</Steps>

### Understanding Stock Information

- **Current Stock**: The actual quantity on hand
- **Par Level**: The target stock level for this item
- **Stock Value**: The total value of current stock
- **Unit of Measure**: How the stock is measured (e.g., kg, liters, units)

## Recording Stock Deliveries

When stock arrives from a supplier, record it in the system:

<Steps>
<Step title="Go to Stock Deliveries">
  Navigate to Stock Management, then select "Deliveries" from the menu.
</Step>

<Step title="Create New Delivery">
  Click the **+** icon to create a new delivery record.
</Step>

<Step title="Enter Delivery Details">
  Fill in the delivery information:
  - Supplier name
  - Delivery date
  - Expected items and quantities
  - Delivery reference number (when required)
</Step>

<Step title="Record Received Items">
  When the delivery arrives, update the record with:
  - Actual quantities received
  - Any discrepancies from expected quantities
  - Condition of items received
</Step>

<Step title="Complete Delivery">
  Review all information and mark the delivery as complete. Stock levels will be updated automatically.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Tip>
Record deliveries as soon as they arrive to keep your inventory levels accurate and up-to-date.
</Tip>

## Transferring Stock

You can transfer stock between different locations:

<Steps>
<Step title="Navigate to Stock Transfers">
  Go to Stock Management and select "Transfers" or "Stock Movements".
</Step>

<Step title="Create Transfer">
  Click the **+** icon to start a stock transfer.
</Step>

<Step title="Select Source Location">
  Choose where the stock is coming from (site, outlet, or preparation point).
</Step>

<Step title="Select Destination">
  Choose where the stock is going to.
</Step>

<Step title="Select Items and Quantities">
  Choose which items to transfer and enter the quantities for each.
</Step>

<Step title="Complete Transfer">
  Review the transfer details and confirm. Stock levels will be updated at both locations.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Warning>
Ensure you have permission to transfer stock between the selected locations. Some transfers may require approval.
</Warning>

## Making Stock Adjustments

Sometimes you need to manually adjust stock levels to account for:

- Spoilage or waste
- Theft or loss
- Counting errors
- Other discrepancies

<Steps>
<Step title="Go to Stock Adjustments">
  Navigate to Stock Management and select "Adjustments".
</Step>

<Step title="Create Adjustment">
  Click the **+** icon to create a stock adjustment.
</Step>

<Step title="Select Location and Item">
  Choose the location and stock item you want to adjust.
</Step>

<Step title="Enter Adjustment Details">
  Provide:
  - The quantity adjustment (positive to add, negative to subtract)
  - Reason for the adjustment
  - Any additional notes
</Step>

<Step title="Save Adjustment">
  Review and save the adjustment. Stock levels will be updated immediately.
</Step>
</Steps>

## Performing Stock Takes

Stock takes help you verify and reconcile your inventory:

<Steps>
<Step title="Create Stock Take Period">
  Go to Stock Management and select "Stock Takes". Click the **+** icon to create a new stock take period.
</Step>

<Step title="Define Scope">
  Select which location(s) and items you want to count. You can count:
  - All items at a location
  - Specific product groups
  - Individual items
</Step>

<Step title="Record Counts">
  For each item, enter the actual quantity you count. The system will show:
  - Expected quantity (from system records)
  - Counted quantity (what you enter)
  - Variance (difference between expected and counted)
</Step>

<Step title="Review Discrepancies">
  The system highlights items where the counted quantity differs from expected. Review these carefully.
</Step>

<Step title="Reconcile Stock">
  Approve adjustments for discrepancies. The system will update stock levels to match your counts.
</Step>
</Steps>

<Info>
Stock takes are essential for maintaining accurate inventory records. Perform them regularly according to your business needs.
</Info>

## Stock Dashboard

The Stock Dashboard provides a real-time overview of your inventory:

- **Current Stock Levels**: See stock levels across all locations
- **Low Stock Alerts**: Items that are below par level
- **Recent Movements**: Latest deliveries, transfers, and adjustments
- **Stock Value**: Total value of inventory
- **Trend Analysis**: Stock level trends over time

The dashboard updates automatically, so you always see current information.

## Stock Reports

Access detailed stock reports to analyze your inventory:

- **Stock Level Reports**: Current stock by location, product, or product group
- **Movement History**: Track all stock movements over time
- **Delivery Summaries**: Review delivery records and supplier performance
- **Stock Valuation**: See the total value of your inventory
- **Variance Reports**: Identify discrepancies and trends
